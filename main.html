Munnar, Kerala


Munnar is a serene hill station nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India. Known for its lush tea plantations, misty valleys, and cool climate, it’s a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. The town sits at the confluence of three rivers and offers a tranquil escape from the bustle of city life.

  1. Eravikulam National Park — Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and stunning grasslands.
  2. Mattupetty Dam — A scenic reservoir ideal for boating and wildlife spotting.
  3. Top Station — Offers panoramic views of the Western Ghats and the rare Neelakurinji blooms.
  4. Tea Museum — Learn about the history and art of tea production in Munnar.
  5. Attukal Waterfalls — A picturesque cascade perfect for trekking and photography.
  • Appam with Stew — A soft rice pancake paired with aromatic vegetable or meat stew.
  • Kerala Sadya — A traditional feast served on banana leaves with multiple vegetarian dishes.
  • Malabar Parotta — Flaky flatbread best enjoyed with spicy curry.
  • Fish Moilee — A mild coconut-based curry with fresh local fish.
  • Cardamom Tea — A fragrant brew made from locally grown spices.
